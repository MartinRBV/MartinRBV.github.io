Thorough Cleansing

Martin Rafael B. Villalon
Cecil Rhodes once said, “Man begets, but land does not beget”. Overpopulation is a pervasive issue in the Philippines. The country had a population of 106,651,922 people in 2018 (an increase of 1,731,922 people compared to 2017). In 2015, the combined population of Metropolitan Manila’s 16 cities was 11,553,427 people. Manila, the capital of the Philippines, has a population density of 46,000 people per square mile-twice the density of New York City-while also boasting a fertility rate of 3.1 children per woman. These numbers are considerably high, and will only keep rising to the point it gets out of control. There is, however, a clear solution to the whole problem: abortions. Abortions are procedures that end a pregnancy by removing the fetus and placenta from the uterus. The idea is simple yet effective. If the baby is unwanted, then delete it before it comes out from between the legs. Brilliant! Sadly, numerous religions, namely Roman Catholic, prohibit this act because it goes against their “moral code” and the mere mention of it is a “mortal flaw”. No matter how we look at it, religious ideas are preventing us from tackling the root of the issue. Thus, they have become the new source of this problem and must be dealt with accordingly. If so, why don’t we kill all the Roman Catholics?

Roman Catholics comprise more than 86% of the Philippine population. Ergo, by wiping the religion clean off the face of the country, we have effectively removed those who go against innovation and progress. But how will we put off that many people? Simply put the country under martial law once again. We might have to ice some Muslims and Christians as well, but all of this will be dealt with swiftly and efficiently thanks to military power. Once we reach the appropriate kill count, the remainder that survived this purge is now free to have abortions whenever they want. There will be no more political nor religious influence to stop them from taking contraceptives. However, this may seem like a very bland solution to the issue. Fortunately, there is a much more entertaining alternative than just implementing martial law.

If we desire more fun in our lives, look no further than battle royals. Battle royals are fights where numerous combatants engage in battle until only one remains standing. It’s obviously not amusing to just watch people get killed, so why not watch people kill each other with passion? We don’t even need to set up a battlefield because the lands of the Philippines are sufficient enough! Once we have sorted the Roman Catholics from the rest of the population, we must take the loved ones of the competitors in our game. This will assure that they fight to the death and have a reason for doing so. If we don’t reach our desired kill count on time, we can start slaying some of the captives to further motivate their drive. Same as the previous solution, the remaining population is now free to have as many abortions as their hearts desire because religious and political influences are out of the picture. Bloody fists, blazing guns, shining knives, and a front-row seat to the best massacre since the Holocaust. What a wonderful sight to behold!


What’s that? Mass genocide is not effective and will only cause chaos and destruction!? Yes, that is correct. These solutions are obviously over-the-top and only bring damage to the country without proving anything other than inhumanity. What we really need to do is spread sex education (sex ed). The poorest social groups are incredibly ignorant, so much so that some do not know how or why a woman becomes pregnant and have not even heard of contraceptives before. Also, as a devoutly religious country, the Philippines is significantly influenced by church morals. Because of this, there are reduced government campaigns on pregnancy prevention. This is where sex ed comes in. Sex education is high-quality teaching and learning of a variety of topics related to sex, sexuality, and skills in managing one’s own sexual health. Informing people and teaching them about the risks of having sex and the possible solutions to unwanted children can make a huge difference in a growing population. All we need to do is be more open-minded about these issues and approach them with care and intent.

In the end, overpopulation is an age-old problem that keeps on getting shunned every year. It can’t be solved without first accepting facts and basing appropriate actions upon those facts. Remember that land does not beget. There won’t be enough land to sustain future generations if the population keeps growing. Will we ever look past our ignorance and pride for the sake of our country’s development?
